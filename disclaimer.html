Force Majeure Events:

  • Natural Disasters: Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions, and other natural catastrophes.
  • Pandemics: Disease outbreaks, epidemics, pandemics, and related government restrictions.
  • Government Actions: Laws, regulations, orders, embargoes, sanctions, or other government actions.
  • War and Conflict: War, terrorism, civil unrest, riots, or military actions.
  • Infrastructure Failures: Widespread power outages, telecommunications failures, internet disruptions, or infrastructure breakdowns.

  • Labor Disputes: Strikes, lockouts, or labor disputes affecting critical services.
  • Other Events: Any other event beyond the reasonable control of the affected party.

Effect of Force Majeure:

  • Suspension: Performance obligations are suspended during force majeure events.
  • No Liability: Neither party is liable for delays or failures caused by force majeure events.
  • Notification: The affected party should notify the other party promptly.
  • Mitigation: The affected party should take reasonable steps to mitigate the impact.
  • Extended Events: If a force majeure event continues for more than 60 days, either party may terminate the affected project without liability.

What Is NOT Force Majeure:

  • Ordinary business risks or market changes.
  • Financial difficulties or inability to pay.
  • Personnel changes on either side.
  • Changes in business priorities or strategy.
  • Ordinary technical issues that could have been prevented.
  • Third-party service changes that could have been anticipated.

Mutual Protection: Force majeure clauses protect both parties. Neither we nor you can be held responsible for events completely outside human control. If such events significantly affect a project, we will work together to find fair solutions, which may include timeline adjustments, scope modifications, or in rare cases, termination with prorated settlement.
